Section 1

What Is the Coherence Scale Measuring?

The degree of alignment between a person's inner life and their outer expression.

What Is Coherence?

Living as Who You Truly Are

Coherence is the experience of living in a way where what you think, what you feel, what you believe, and what you value are genuinely reflected in what you say, how you relate, and what you do.

What coherence is NOT

  • Not the same as happiness
  • Not social conformity or agreeableness
  • Not about being pleasant

What coherence IS

  • Inner-outer alignment
  • Felt congruence and integrity
  • Being real, not performing
What Is Coherence?

Theoretical Foundations

Carl Rogers

Concept of congruence: alignment between ideal self, real self, and presented self as a foundation of psychological health.

Self-Determination Theory

Deci & Ryan: autonomous, self-concordant behavior — acting from one's own values rather than external pressure — is essential to well-being.

Authenticity Research

Wood, Linley, Maltby & Baliousis: measuring the gap between authentic inner experience and outward self-expression.

Existential Philosophy

Living in alignment with chosen values and confronting the temptation to live inauthentically.

Section 2

Why Measure Coherence?

Filling the research gap with a unified framework across speech, relationships, and action.

Why Measure Coherence?

The Research Gap

Existing instruments measure related constructs — authenticity, self-concordance, well-being, values congruence — but no single instrument captures the full picture of inner-outer alignment across all domains simultaneously.

Section 3

The Four Domains of Coherence

How a person feels, speaks, relates, and acts.

Four Domains

The Coherence Framework

Domain 1 • 14 self-report items

Well-Being

The degree of positive psychological functioning: optimism, energy, confidence, clarity, feeling loved and interested in life.

Domain 2 • 8 self-report items

Speech Alignment

Whether spoken words match genuine thoughts and feelings. Honest opinions, consistent messaging, saying what one means without censoring for others' reactions.

Domain 3 • 8 self-report items

Relationship Alignment

Whether relational behavior reflects the authentic self. Showing who you truly are, not curating a version, not maintaining connections from obligation.

Domain 4 • 11 self-report items

Action Alignment

Whether daily actions, choices, habits, and goals reflect stated values. The most concrete and observable expression of inner-outer congruence.

Survey D

Weekly Coherence Pulse

5 items. 10 seconds. Every week. Tracking the trajectory of change.

"Take a breath. Reflect on this past week. Answer quickly and honestly — your first instinct is the right one."

What the Pulse Data Reveals

  • Which program module produced the largest shift in each domain
  • Whether certain domains shift before others
  • Whether progress is steady, stepwise, or includes dips before gains
  • Individual patterns for personalized coaching
